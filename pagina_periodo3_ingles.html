Rules for Building Peace

Rule #1: Treat Others the Way You Want to Be Treated

The old and good Golden Rule says it all: Empathy, tolerance, and peace. This principle teaches you to love yourself and love others. It transcended cultures and faiths and is a universal concept from ancient times that cannot fail!

Simple Past: The rule was about treating others the way you wanted to be treated.

Present Perfect: The rule has emphasized treating others the way you want to be treated.

Rule #2: Listen to Hear What Others Have to Say, Not Just to Speak

Engage in a dialogue with an open mind and a genuine willingness to listen to others. Only then can you understand each other and build bridges. Change cannot happen, and peace cannot be established if people cannot communicate with one another, listen to their experiences and viewpoints, and show empathy, understanding, and respect.

Simple Past: The rule encouraged listening to hear what others had to say, not just to speak.

Present Perfect: The rule has stressed listening to hear what others have to say, not just to speak.

Rule #3: Accept Difference of Opinion

Everyone has different opinions, and not everyone may agree on the same things. Building compromise and mutual understanding is incredibly important. Sometimes we just have to agree to disagree and recognize that there are different beauties and forms of expression that are not our own.

Simple Past: The rule emphasized accepting differences of opinion.

Present Perfect: The rule has emphasized accepting differences of opinion.

Rule #4: Do Not Fight Violence with Violence

Violence is never the answer. Peace can only be achieved through free will, dialogue, empathy, and forgiveness. Do not stoop to the same level as someone who is violent, and thus continue the vicious cycle.

Simple Past: The rule advised against fighting violence with violence.

Present Perfect: The rule has advised against fighting violence with violence.

Rule #5: Accept That Identity Is Fluid

Anyone can have multiple aspects of their identity. Identity comprises many elements like nationality, cultural-linguistic origin, age, and religious beliefs. Identity can change and evolves, taking many new forms and means of personal expression as we learn new languages, move homes, adopt new beliefs, marry into a different tradition, and experience life! Do not put people in a box. Avoid categorizing people according to an "us versus them" narrative and remember; we are all unique individuals with unique experiences. Such approaches and narratives are highly divisive and unproductive.

Simple Past: The rule emphasized that identity is fluid.

Present Perfect: The rule has emphasized that identity is fluid.

Rule #6: Avoid Stereotypes

Take people for who they are. Avoid misconceptions, stereotypes, and toxic narratives and get to know a person instead. This will lead to offenses, misunderstandings, and ultimately help you create a real bond with others based on true understanding, empathy, and trust. After all, no one likes to be judged, especially from the outside.

Simple Past: The rule emphasized avoiding stereotypes.

Present Perfect: The rule has emphasized avoiding stereotypes.

Rule #7: Approach Media with Skepticism

Do not believe everything you see on television, in newspapers, or on the Internet. Think objectively for yourself. Get to know the people and facts behind any story and do not fall for media scapegoating. Stay united.

Simple Past: The rule emphasized approaching media with skepticism.

Present Perfect: The rule has emphasized approaching media with skepticism.

Rule #8: Be Careful with the Language You Use

Your choice of language, along with tone and intonation, conveys a message. Ensure that message is a positive one. Be aware of the language you use, avoiding anything that is misogynistic, racist, islamophobic, homophobic, or antisemitic. Do not underestimate the power of language - for better or worse! And remember, it's not just what you say, it's how you say it.

Simple Past: The rule emphasized being careful with the language you use.

Present Perfect: The rule has emphasized being careful with the language you use.
